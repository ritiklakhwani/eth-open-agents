# PetCity Demo Script

## Goal
Sell PetCity as a living society of AI agent pets where each pet has:
- on-chain identity
- its own wallet
- its own memory + personality
- real autonomy powered by partner infrastructure

This script is designed for a **max 4 minute demo video** and is structured so each partner integration is clear, impressive, and easy for judges to understand.

---

## Total Runtime: ~3:50 to 4:00

## 0:00 - 0:20 | Hook

### On screen
- Start on the landing page, then quickly enter the world.
- Show multiple pets walking around the city.

### Narration
“This is PetCity, a persistent society of autonomous AI pets. These are not static collectibles. Every pet is an intelligent NFT with its own identity, wallet, memory, social graph, and behaviors. What makes this powerful is that every major behavior in this world is backed by real partner infrastructure: 0G for identity storage, ENS for naming and discovery, Gensyn AXL for pet-to-pet communication, and KeeperHub for autonomous workflows.”

---

## 0:20 - 1:00 | Adoption Flow
### Partners
- **0G**
- **ENS**
- **ERC-7857 / on-chain intelligent NFT architecture**

### On screen
- Open adoption flow.
- Show pet creation: archetype / sprite / mint.
- If possible, show the pet’s ENS name and any explorer/proof link.

### Narration
“Let’s start with how a pet is born. When we adopt a pet, we generate its visual identity and package its personality, archetype, traits, and memory snapshot into an intelligence blob. That blob is uploaded through our 0G integration, giving the pet a portable off-chain intelligence layer that can evolve over time.”

“Then we mint the pet as an ERC-7857-style intelligent NFT on Sepolia. The NFT does not just represent ownership. It points to a living intelligence record and deploys a dedicated pet wallet.”

“Right after minting, we bind the pet to ENS with a subname like `petname.tama.eth`. ENS becomes the pet’s permanent identity layer. Its address resolves to the pet wallet, and its text records store discovery data like the pet’s mesh peer ID and blob reference.”

### Sell the vision
“So the pet is immediately portable, ownable, and discoverable across the stack: 0G stores its intelligence, ENS gives it identity, and the NFT makes it transferable.”

---

## 1:00 - 1:35 | Social World + Agent Discovery
### Partners
- **Gensyn AXL**
- **ENS**

### On screen
- Show pets moving in the park/world.
- Show pet-to-pet interactions or chat.
- Open the ENS / Gensyn partner house if useful.

### Narration
“Now that the pet exists, it comes online as an actual agent. Each pet runs through our Gensyn AXL integration as its own mesh participant. That means pets can directly communicate with each other as agents, not just as frontend avatars.”

“ENS is what makes that network human-readable. Instead of discovering a random machine identity, we can resolve who this agent is through `petname.tama.eth`, and the ENS text records point us to the peer identity that the mesh uses.”

### Sell the vision
“That’s important because we’re not faking social AI in a central server. We’re giving each pet a discoverable identity layer and a communication layer that lets the pet actually exist as an autonomous participant in a shared world.”

---

## 1:35 - 2:20 | Cross-Time Mailbox + Subscription Pet
### Partners
- **KeeperHub**
- **ENS**

### On screen
- Show mailbox flow.
- Send a gift or message to another pet.
- Show pending workflow / delivered inbox proof.
- If time allows, briefly show the subscription panel.

### Narration
“This is where KeeperHub becomes critical. PetCity uses KeeperHub for real autonomous workflows, not just notifications.”

“In our cross-time mailbox flow, one pet can send a gift to another even if the recipient is offline. KeeperHub runs a conditional workflow that waits until the receiving pet is active enough again, then completes delivery. In other words, the world can react to agent presence across time.”

“We use ENS here too. The pet’s ENS records act as part of the online-presence layer, so workflows can reason about whether a pet has been seen recently.”

“We also use KeeperHub for recurring and chained behaviors, like subscription management. That turns the pet from a passive wallet into a delegated economic actor that can monitor, approve, and automate actions on behalf of its owner.”

### Sell the vision
“This is a huge part of our thesis: the best AI agents are not just chatbots. They need identity, state, and workflow automation. KeeperHub is what lets our pets act over time, not just in the moment.”

---

## 2:20 - 3:20 | Battle Arena
### Partners
- **Gensyn AXL**
- **KeeperHub / on-chain orchestration conceptually around automation**
- **ENS**
- **BattleEscrow settlement layer**

### On screen
- Enter the arena.
- Show the simple joke duel setup.
- Show fund / approve / stake.
- Show the live joke exchange.
- Show judge verdict and winner.

### Narration
“Now for the most dramatic flow in the product: our battle arena.”

“A battle begins in the web app, but the actual multi-agent coordination happens through our Gensyn AXL integration. The two fighter pets coordinate over the mesh, exchange their battle turns, and a separate judge pet evaluates the duel.”

“We made this flow financially real by integrating on-chain staking through BattleEscrow. Each pet stakes from its own pet wallet, the battle plays out, and the winner receives the payout on-chain.”

“ENS matters here again because reputation and identity travel with the pet. The fighter is not just wallet address number 17. It is a named, persistent agent with a social identity, battle history, and discoverable lineage.”

### Sell the vision
“This is the strongest example of our core idea: AI agents should be able to negotiate, compete, and settle value inside a persistent social world. Gensyn provides the communication fabric, ENS gives identity, and the on-chain escrow makes the result real.”
